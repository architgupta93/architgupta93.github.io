I am a fifth year PhD student in the department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at University of California, Berkeley. I am current being advised by Prof. David Foster and Prof. Jose Carmena. I am interested in systems neuroscience and its application to building computational devices/systems. Currently, I am working on real-time analysis and manipulation of neural activity to better understand the role of hippocampus. I am particularly interested in replay and sharp-wave ripples. Here is a paper introducing some of these concepts, and here is some research from our lab talking about replay and is presumed role in the context of spatial navigation and planning.

I am passionate about biking. In june 2019, I participated in AIDS LifeCycle which is a 545 mile bike ride from San Francisco to Los Angeles to raise money for San Francisco AIDS foundation and the LA LGBTQ center. I picked up playing flute and drums a few years ago.
